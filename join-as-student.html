Join MIS as a Student

Thank you for your interest in joining Mission International School as a student.

MIS offers a curriculum based on the Ghana Education Service, Nursery, Primary & Secondary programme leading to the BECE and WASSCE examination. We also have a special pathway in Music and Creative Media for students aged 13 years and above (i.e. JHS 1 upwards).

Infant School pupils (Nursery - Class 3) follow the GES curriculum, learning Writing, Numeracy, and specialist subject experiences in Art, P.E., Creative Arts, Computing, Music, and French. The curriculum is holistic, focusing on the whole child and connecting their development with the host country.

Very soon, we will also provide an enriching boarding experience for students in Senior High school (Form 1-3). It would be a weekly boarding offering where students are required to return home at the end of each week (Fridays) and report back to the boarding house on Sunday afternoons (ready for the start of each school week).

Please refer to our FAQ document, our Admission Age Guide and Boarding Programme Prospectus for additional information.

THE STUDENT ADMISSIONS PROCESS

Stage 1: Completion of Forms

Parental Enquiry Form and Application Form

  • These forms are available in digital versions through the Office of Admissions & International Partnerships and must be completed and submitted digitally to the Office. The Office does not accept printed, handwritten, scanned, or photographed copies of the form.
  • The registration fee can be paid online or paid at a bank, into the MIS Account. The finance department will also accept POS (point of sale) payment for the registration fee only. No other payment is required at this point. The fee is GHC100 for all applications (Nursery-JHS 3).

NB: The School is unable to proceed with an application without evidence of successful completion of all requirements under step 1 above.

Once the completed Application Form is received, the file undergoes a thorough review by the Admissions Team.

Stage 2: Entrance Assessments

All admissions into Mission International School are subject to a successful entrance assessment.

  • English is the language of instruction, therefore, entrance exams are conducted in the English Language.
  • Applicants for primary 3 to JHS 3 are assessed in Maths and English.
  • Prospective SHS students are assessed in Maths, English, Combined Science (Biology, Chemistry, Physics), and Spanish.
  • Entry assessments for late entrants (international applicants) may be conducted in August or as required.

Stage 3: Acceptance/Denial of Placement

The decision to make an offer of admission is always made by the Principal.

  • After the assessment, interview, and submission of confidential student reports, Parents will receive a notification by email on whether their child will be offered a place or not.

Stage 4: Submission of Documentation and Payment of Fees

  • Once the child has been offered admission, parents are informed of all mandatory documentation including medical reports, rules and regulations document, food allergy forms, etc., and given a deadline for submission.
  • Upon receipt of the required documentation, admission and tuition fee invoices will be issued for payment.

Stage 5: Admissions Prospectus and Orientation

Following a successful enrollment, additional information on next steps will be provided. The Office of Admissions & International Partnerships organises orientation events for all new families.

*Please note that all monetary transactions must be made to the School's account. Proof of all payments must be shared with the School.

MIS accepts deferred admission offers for students who have been offered a place but wish to defer their start date. Deferred offers are valid for one academic year. Please contact the Office of Admissions & International Partnerships for more information.

What age groups does MIS accept?
MIS accepts students from Nursery to SHS 3.

Is there a waiting list?
Yes, when year groups are full, applicants are placed on a waiting list and contacted when a place becomes available.

Can international students apply?
Absolutely. MIS welcomes students of all nationalities. International applicants may have their entrance assessments conducted in their country of residence.
