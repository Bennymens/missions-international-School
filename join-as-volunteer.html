Join MIS as a Volunteer

Thank you for your interest in volunteering at Mission International School.

Mission International School values the contributions of volunteers who are passionate about education and making a positive impact in the lives of young learners. Volunteering at MIS offers a unique opportunity to be part of a vibrant, multicultural school community.

Volunteers play an essential role in supporting school programmes, enriching the student experience, and contributing to the wider school community. Whether you have a few hours a week or can commit to a longer engagement, your contribution is welcomed and valued.

MIS provides a safe, supportive environment for all volunteers and ensures that your time with us is both meaningful and rewarding.

THE VOLUNTEER APPLICATION PROCESS

Stage 1: Expression of Interest

Prospective volunteers are invited to submit the following to the Office of Admissions & International Partnerships:

  • A completed MIS Volunteer Application Form (available digitally through the Office of Admissions & International Partnerships).
  • A brief statement outlining your motivation for volunteering, areas of interest, and any relevant skills or experience.
  • A copy of valid identification (passport or national ID).
  • Contact details of at least two references who can speak to your character and suitability for working with children.

Stage 2: Screening and Background Checks

All volunteer applications undergo a thorough review to ensure the safety and wellbeing of our students:

  • Background and criminal record checks are mandatory for all volunteers.
  • Reference checks will be conducted with the contacts provided.
  • The school reserves the right to decline any volunteer application without providing reasons.

Stage 3: Interview and Placement

Shortlisted candidates will be invited for an informal interview:

  • The interview aims to match your skills and interests with available volunteer opportunities within the school.
  • Volunteers are placed in areas that best utilise their expertise, such as classroom assistance, library support, sports coaching, arts programmes, or administrative tasks.
  • International volunteers may be interviewed via video conferencing.

Stage 4: Acceptance and Agreement

  • Successful applicants will receive a confirmation letter outlining the terms of the volunteer engagement, including schedule, responsibilities, and duration.
  • All volunteers are required to sign a Volunteer Agreement and adhere to the school's Child Protection Policy and Code of Conduct.

Stage 5: Orientation

All new volunteers participate in an orientation session covering school policies, safeguarding procedures, and an introduction to the MIS community. Volunteers are also introduced to their supervising staff member who will provide ongoing support.

Interested? Send your expression of interest to missionsinternationalschool@gmail.com

Classroom Support: Assist teachers with classroom activities, reading programmes, and one-on-one student support.

Sports & Activities: Help coach sports teams, supervise after-school clubs, or lead enrichment activities.

Library & Media: Support library operations, help with reading programmes, or assist with school media projects.

Events & Administration: Assist with school events, fundraising activities, and general administrative tasks.

Mentorship: Serve as a mentor to students, providing guidance and support in specific academic or personal development areas.

How much time do I need to commit?
Volunteer commitments are flexible and can range from a few hours per week to full-time engagement. We work with you to find a schedule that suits both your availability and the school's needs.

Do I need teaching experience?
No. While teaching experience is valued, it is not required. We welcome volunteers with diverse skills and backgrounds. Training and orientation are provided.

Can international volunteers apply?
Yes. MIS welcomes volunteers from all nationalities. International volunteers are responsible for their own visa arrangements, though the school can provide supporting documentation where needed.
